What We're Fighting

About Cholangiocarcinoma

Cholangiocarcinoma (CCA) is a rare cancer of the bile ducts. It is often diagnosed late, which makes awareness and research funding critical. Here's what everyone should know.

🔬

What Is It?

CCA forms in the bile ducts — the tubes that carry bile from the liver to the small intestine. It is aggressive and often asymptomatic until advanced stages.

⚠️

Why It's Hard to Detect

Symptoms like jaundice, weight loss, and abdominal pain often appear only in later stages, making early diagnosis rare. There is no routine screening test.

💊

Current Treatments

Standard treatment includes gemcitabine + cisplatin + durvalumab. Targeted therapies exist for specific mutations (FGFR2, IDH1, BRAF), and new trials are ongoing.

🧬

Genomic Testing Matters

Comprehensive genomic profiling (e.g. FoundationOne) can identify actionable mutations and open doors to targeted therapies and clinical trials that standard tests miss.

🏥

Seek Specialist Centers

Peter MacCallum Cancer Centre (Melbourne), Chris O'Brien Lifehouse (Sydney), Royal Prince Alfred Hospital, and Princess Alexandra Hospital (Brisbane) have dedicated cancer programs with bile duct expertise. Remote second opinions are available.

🌐

Community & Research

The Cholangiocarcinoma Foundation leads global research efforts and offers patient registries, trial matching, and peer community support for patients and families.
